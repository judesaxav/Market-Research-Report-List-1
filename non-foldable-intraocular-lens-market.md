<p><p>Non-foldable intraocular lenses (IOLs) are rigid devices used in cataract surgeries to replace the eye's natural lens after its removal. Unlike foldable IOLs, which can be inserted through smaller incisions, non-foldable lenses require larger incisions due to their rigid structure. These lenses are mainly made of materials such as PMMA and are known for their durability and optical clarity.</p><p>The Non-foldable Intraocular Lens Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4% during the forecast period, driven by the rising prevalence of cataracts globally and an increasing aging population. Technological advancements, such as improved lens designs and the introduction of multifocal and toric lenses, are also contributing to market growth. Additionally, the growing awareness and accessibility of eye care services are fostering demand for cataract surgery, thus boosting the non-foldable IOL segment.</p><p>Market trends indicate a shift towards enhanced patient outcomes, focusing on the development of premium lenses that provide better visual acuity and reduce dependency on glasses. Enhanced surgical techniques and collaborations among healthcare providers and manufacturers are further shaping this market, leading to innovative solutions for managing cataracts.</p></p>
