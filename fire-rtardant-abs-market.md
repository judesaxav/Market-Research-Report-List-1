<p><p>The Fire Retardant ABS market is categorized into two primary types: non-halogen and halogen-based products. Non-halogen type fire retardants utilize alternative materials to achieve flame resistance, making them environmentally friendly and less toxic. In contrast, halogen type fire retardants incorporate halogen elements (such as bromine or chlorine), which effectively suppress flames but may release harmful substances when burned. Each type serves distinct applications, with non-halogen products favored in sectors prioritizing safety and sustainability, while halogen products offer cost-effective solutions in various industries.</p></p>
